Roux

Description

Silky smooth roux (prounced roo) not only thickens sauces, soups, and stews, but it also adds a subtle nutty flavor to the dish. It's an essential building block of recipes ranging from macaroni and cheese to gumbo. Every cook should know how to make a roux, and it's easier than you might think. Let's do some roux!

Ingridients

  • 1 part oil or fat
  • 1 part all-purpose flour, by weight.

Steps

  1. Begin by heating 2 tablespoons oil or fat in a saucepan over medium heat until a pinch of flour sprinkled into the oil just begins to bubble.
  2. Then, whisk in 3-1/2 tablespoons of flour to form a thick paste the consistency of cake frosting.
  3. Continue whisking as the roux gently bubbles and cooks to the shade desired. Do not allow the roux to bubble too vigorously, or it will burn rather than brown.
